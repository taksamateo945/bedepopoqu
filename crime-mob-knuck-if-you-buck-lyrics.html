About

Crime Mob was an American hip-hop group formed in Atlanta, Georgia, in 2004. The original members of the group were M.I.G., Cyco Black, Diamond, Princess, and Lil’ Jay. They gained prominence with their debut self-titled album, “Crime Mob,” which was released in the same year.

The group’s music was known for its energetic beats, catchy hooks, and aggressive lyrics. They were associated with the crunk and snap music genres, which were popular in the early 2000s. Their music often featured themes of street life, partying, and female empowerment.

Crime Mob achieved mainstream success with their hit single “Knuck If You Buck,” which became a club anthem and garnered significant attention. The song’s popularity led to the group signing a recording contract with Universal Records.

Following the release of their debut album, Crime Mob released their second studio album, “Hated on Mostly,” in 2007. The album spawned singles like “Rock Yo Hips” featuring Lil Scrappy and “Circles.” While it didn’t achieve the same level of commercial success as their debut, it solidified their presence in the Southern rap scene.
